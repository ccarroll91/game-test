Christmas at Gran's

A poem by Michael Brennan

Christmas at Gran's
We're going to Gran's for Christmas

There will be lots of things to eat

There will be lots of things to eat

Like Christmas pudding and mince pies

Like Christmas pudding and mince pies

And many other tasty treats

And many other tasty treats

This time I was eager again

This time I was eager again

What would the surprise be this year?

What would the surprise be this year?

So I sat down by the table

So I sat down by the table

And Grandma said "Have some sprouts dear."

And Grandma said "Have some sprouts dear."

I felt sick just looking at them

I felt sick just looking at them

But then a thought came in my head

But then a thought came in my head

I sneaked out to the bathroom

I sneaked out to the bathroom

And they went down the toilet instead

And they went down the toilet instead

I saw my sister called Kate

I saw my sister called Kate

Who had her plate shiny and clean

Who had her plate shiny and clean

But then I saw our dog, Bonzo

But then I saw our dog, Bonzo

Who had his mouth smeared with green!

Who had his mouth smeared with green!

Tom had stuffed sprouts down his socks

Tom had stuffed sprouts down his socks

And slipped out through the door

And slipped out through the door

And naughty baby Stephanie

And naughty baby Stephanie

Had thrown them on the floor!

Had thrown them on the floor!

Gran was good and had ate hers up

Gran was good and had ate hers up

Or so it seemed from my view

Or so it seemed from my view

For when she picked up her handbag...

For when she picked up her handbag...

... What rolled out... Was no doubt...

... What rolled out... as no doubt...

... a sprout!

... a sprout!
Michael Brennan and Conor Carroll

The team

Michael Brennan, the author

Conor Carroll, the artist

Christmas at Gran's was written by Michael Brennan at the age of 10, and adapted into this digital story by Conor Carroll. It is with deep regret that Conor discovered that Bonzo did not, in fact, exist, and was a figment of Brennan's imagination for the sake of the poem. As Michael's brother Tom put it, he took "a bit of creative licence there". We hope that you love him all the same.
